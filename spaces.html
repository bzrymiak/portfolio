01.

Project Overview

This case study is still in progress! Check back later or reach out for more information!

For SparkJam 2026, a 5-day design sprint hosted by SFU Surge, my team and I decide to build on discord following the prompt “add an creation-based feature to a non-creation software,” meaning that we would need to find a way to create an interesting and meaningful customization-based addition to Discord that simultaneously felt native to the software.

Our pitch video can be viewed on YouTube and our full prototype on Figma.

02.

Objective

Our mission was to develop a feature for Discord to help bridge that gap between real and digital spaces. Inspired by digital office spaces for remote workers, we developed the idea of rooms within Discord's voice calls (VCs), where users could move around, customize their call's space, and play games that already exist in VCs.

After some research and feedback, we decided to pivot our design to something that felt native to Discord, resulting in a communal and customizable canvas within VCs full of widgets, stickers, GIFs, custom backgrounds, call thumbnail customization, and a feature to save your settings in a template.

03.

Outcome

Widgets, including a Spotify player.

The customizable background feature.

04.

Takeaways

I learned the importance of sticking to user research, in order to avoid designing for ourselves like we did initially. I also learned the difference that good communication and clear teamwork structure has on a project, which allowed all of us to experiment and learn from each other while still being able to showcase our individual strengths.
